About Me

I am a dedicated and experienced educator with over 8 years of teaching experience, helping students from Classes 1 to 8 build strong academic foundations.

I hold a Master’s degree in Computer Applications (MCA) and a Bachelor’s degree in Science (Non-Medical). Over the years, I have worked closely with students from different academic backgrounds, including convent school students, and understand the importance of structured learning and discipline.

My teaching approach focuses on concept clarity rather than rote learning. I ensure that each student understands the “why” behind every topic, especially in subjects like Mathematics and Science, where strong fundamentals are essential for long-term success.

I believe every child learns differently, which is why I maintain small batch sizes (5–6 students) to provide personal attention and customized support. Regular tests, doubt-solving sessions, and continuous feedback help track progress and build confidence.

My goal is not just to help students score better marks, but to develop their interest in learning, improve problem-solving skills, and build confidence that stays with them throughout their academic journey.
